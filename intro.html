Hi there,

I am really appreciated that you are offering me this chance to prove my abilities as a web designer. From the test you gave me, I have to admit that it is really challenged but also interesting to work on.

So this is my approach for the test. I assumed that goals I need to achieve are:

  • 1. Make the dataset to be easier to read and analyze.
  • 2. Create a place for the users to read and share their thoughts.
  • 3. Give them some ideas about others.

Please take a look at how it works *make sure to watch in full-screen ;)

It is not responsive yet. But you can see it in action here (the chart) and here (the user profile). I could have made the design to look more fancy but I don't think that will meet the needs of the users. I wanted to keep it at this simple so I can really achieve my goals to help the users have a better engagement with the product. If you want to read more about the process, keep reading.

The Process

First, To make the dataset to be easier to read and analyze, I think one of the good way is to visualize it into charts which can be easy for the users to customize so that they can choose exactly what information they want to read from the dataset. So from that given dataset, I think the users can choose what kind of indicators in which country and in which years they want to see. The chart is not working properly yet. But it should be able to display the data for any selected indicator.

Secondly is the comment list. I wanted to make a room where the users can easily write their comments while they can also quickly take a look at the dataset and check its content again whenever they want with no extra actions needed.

Third, to give them some ideas about each other. I made a profile page where they can find some informations of the other users. So they will know who that person is or what contents that person have contributed to the community. At a glance they can quickly preview comments or posts are made. They can click on it to be linked to the main thread for full details too.

That's it. Thank you for reading! Hope you like this!
